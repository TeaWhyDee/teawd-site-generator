Speech to text diary script(s)

#tech #linux

First of all, this blog post is about a somewhat specific problem. Despite this, I think it is quite useful to most readers looking for some Linux knowledge as the tools I used are very general. Let's start with the explanation of

The problem

Sometimes I have good ideas when I'm outside. Sometimes I really wanna preserve these ideas, but it's very inconvenient to use phone keyboards, especially on the move. One solution is recording my voice. But then, I would want to transfer those recordings to my computer for future reference.

That's not too hard to do, but what if I also want to read the text version of those voice recordings?

The solution

Syncthing

Syncthing is an open source cross-platform program that can sync folders accross multiple devices. It's perfect for this problem, as it allows for P2P syncing (for example from your phone to your computer).
It's very easy to set up aswell, you download it on both devices, open a browser tab on 127.0.0.1:8384 and scan the ID QR with your phones camera. Then share whatever is the folder that has your audio recordings.
Sometimes your folder might have some additional files popping up in it, you can configure syncthing on your desktop to ignore those.
If you want more robust syncing, you could also host a Syncthing instance on a server. In case you don't trust the server, Syncthing also provides encryption.
