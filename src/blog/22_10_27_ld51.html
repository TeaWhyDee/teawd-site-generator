For the first time in forever I could completely dedicate myself to game development for entire 3 days during the latest Ludum Dare.

Taking Risks

Me and PizzArt had very little experience making 3D games, so starting one was a pretty bold decision on our part. To save us from complete failure and to greatly simplify development, I decided to make a locked-camera top-down view. This was also our first time inviting a "stranger" to help us with sound-design and music. I believe this was a very important decision.

Designing the game

The theme for the game was "Every 10 seconds". It sucks. We couldn't think of any non-trivial mechanics so instead I decided to focus on art and story. For the character, I came up with the first cute thing that came to mind, a black yellow-eyed cat. The entirety of the first 2 days were spent designing assets and animating the characters. The only mechanic was enemies looking at you every 10 seconds, things felt really dire. I really wanted a more insteresting character, so by the end of day 2 I came up with a genius idea of a constantly sneezing cat. It's both cute and ties together the idea of enemies only being able to see you every 10 seconds.
